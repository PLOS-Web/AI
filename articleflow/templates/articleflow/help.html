Zyg Instructions

Firstly, always login. Your login is the same as your username and password used to login to your computer.

Very simply A.I. consists of two pages — the Homepage and the Grid.

The Homepage is a shortcut to all the articles that are unassigned and awaiting QC. Clicking on the A.I. logo in the upper left hand corner will take you to the Homepage.

To begin QC, simply click on the "Assign to me" button and you will be taken directly to the article's Workarea page.

The Workarea page contains three sections: Issues, Errors, and Change Article State.

In the grey box you will find an area dedicated to information about the article like PubDate, Current State, who it's assigned to, a link to the article on stage, a link to the EM File Inventory, and a link to the PDF on stage. This is also where you can leave a note.

For a more specific view, you can click on the other tabs to see just Issues, Errors etc.

Issues is where you will report issues you find during QC. From the "Select Category" drop down select the area where the issue occurs. This will either be in the PDF, XML, SI or PDF and XML. Write a description of the issue and hit Submit.

Errors is where you will see any errors found during article delivery from CW.

Change Article State is the last step in QC. Once you are finished QCing the article, you have two options to remove the article from your queue. If you did not find any issues, click "OK". If there are issues, click "Needs Web Corrections (CW)".

Once you make your selection, the buttons will disappear and you will see the message "No available transitions". In the grey box, "Assigned to" will be blanked out and the Current state will change from Ready for QC (CW) to Needs Web Corrections (CW) or Ready to Publish.

Click on the A.I. logo to go back to the homepage and assign yourself a new article to QC.

The Grid is your go–to page to find out whatever you need to know about article history, article deliveries, article pub list etc. By default, the Grid will display all the articles that have published starting with the oldest.

That said, you will almost always want to use [+] search to customize the articles you want to see. Clicking on [+] search will display filters you can use to run your Search as well as options to customize the way the Grid is displayed.

Some common things you may want to know are:

  • I want to see articles that are pubbing on (fill in date here):
    • Click on the box next to From and select a date
    • Click on the box next to To and select a date
    • Select a particular journal(s)
    • Select the Article State — in this case it would be "Published on Stage"
    • Hit Search.
  • I want to see what’s going on with a particular article:
    • Enter the DOI of the article (e.g. pone.0012345, pmed.1001234, pntd.0001234)
    • Hit Search.
  • I want to see the articles that have been delivered today:
    • Click on the box next to From and select a date
    • Click on the box next to To and select a date
    • Select the Article State — in this case it would be "Delivered"
    • Hit Search.
  • I want to see the articles assigned to (person's name).
    • From the Assigned scrollbox, select the person's name
    • Hit Search.

Your search will return a list with the following columns:

  • DOI : the article digital object identifier. Clicking on the DOI takes you directly to the article Workarea page
  • PubDate : the date the article will be published, according to EM
  • Journal :
  • Issues : the types of issues (PDF, XML, SI)
  • Errors :
  • State : the step in the production at which the article is currently in
  • Assigned : any related notes
